Use of Thermal Camera for Electromechanical Inspection

Introduction

I learned that this seminar was made possible by the Bicol University Electrical Engineering Department, in partnership with the Institute of Integrated Electrical Engineers - United Arab Emirates Chapter. The event was part of the ProfDev Series titled: Charting the Frontiers of Emerging Technologies in Electrical Engineering. This series aims to engage learners and researchers with ideas, innovations, and impacts in the field of engineering. The first session, "Use of Thermal Camera for Electromechanical Inspection and Troubleshooting," was a virtual technical lecture delivered by Engr. Ferdinand B. Borlongan via Zoom.

Summary

Engr. Ferdinand B. Borlongan truly illuminated the world of thermal cameras! He provided a fantastic overview of how these handy devices are used for electromechanical inspection and troubleshooting, making complex topics surprisingly accessible and engaging. He highlighted how thermal cameras can detect anomalies like overheating components, faulty connections, and insulation defects, preventing costly breakdowns and ensuring operational efficiency. The session also included practical demonstrations and case studies, illustrating the effectiveness of thermal cameras in real-world scenarios.

Learnings

I learned how thermal cameras are essential for detecting anomalies like overheating, faulty connections, and insulation defects in electromechanical systems. Their ability to provide non-contact temperature measurements makes them a powerful tool for preventative maintenance, ensuring safety and operational efficiency. The practical demonstrations and case studies clearly illustrated their effectiveness in real-world applications.

Basic Solar Design and Installation

Introduction

I was able to attend this seminar, Basic Solar Design, with the speaker being Engr. Constantine Reymundo. Although I was a bit late because of the internet outage, I was still able to participate in the interactive quiz at the end.

Summary

The webinar delved into the intricacies of solar design, covering topics such as different types of solar panels, their efficiency ratings, and the key components of a photovoltaic system, including inverters, batteries, and charge controllers. It also touched upon system sizing, site assessment, and the regulatory requirements for solar installations in the Philippines.

Learnings

I learned that solar design is a multi-faceted business that requires significant capital investment, which is why it is not widely adopted yet despite its benefits. The session was very informative on the current trends and the future of solar energy in the Philippines, highlighting both its economic and environmental advantages.
